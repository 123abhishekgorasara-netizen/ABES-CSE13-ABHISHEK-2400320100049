Exploring America

America, also known as the United States of America (USA)—a country that’s as vast in geography as it is in influence: 🇺🇸 United States of America: A Snapshot 🌍 Geography & Size - Located in North America, bordered by Canada to the north and Mexico to the south. - Comprises 50 states, including the non-contiguous states of Alaska and Hawaii. - Third-largest country in the world by both land area and population, with over 340 million people. 🏛️ Government & History - A federal presidential republic, with power divided between the national government and individual states.

  • - The USA is the third-largest country by land area, after Russia and Canada. - It spans six time zones, from UTC−4 to UTC−10. - Denali, in Alaska, is the tallest mountain in North America at 6,190 meters. - Death Valley holds the record for the highest air temperature ever recorded on Earth: 56.7°C (134°F) in 1913. -
  • - The USA declared independence from Britain on July 4, 1776. - It was the first country to gain independence from a European power in modern history (1783). - The U.S. Constitution was adopted in 1788, and Delaware was the first state to ratify it. - Washington, D.C. is the capital but is not a state—it’s a federal district
